The integrated analysis of breast cancer tissue reveals a phenotype driven by two primary, interconnected biological processes: dysregulated cell cycle progression and profound metabolic reprogramming, particularly concerning lipid metabolism. A highly active network of mitotic regulators, including CDK1, CCNB1, AURKA, and CDC20, drives uncontrolled proliferation. This is orchestrated by the master transcription factor FOXM1. Concurrently, the cancer cells exhibit a shift in lipid metabolism, evidenced by the upregulation of genes like PPARG, LIPE, and FABP4, likely providing the necessary energy and biosynthetic materials for rapid growth. Hormonal signaling via androgen and glucocorticoid receptors appears to further integrate these proliferative and metabolic pathways, while changes in the extracellular matrix suggest a potential for increased invasion and metastasis.
