In Peripheral Arterial Disease (PAD), a complex interplay of immune dysregulation, cellular stress, and apoptosis drives vascular pathology. The system is orchestrated by key transcription factors, primarily RELA (NF-κB) and FOS, which activate a network of genes involved in inflammatory signaling (NF-κB, MAPK pathways), innate immunity (TLR4, NLRP3), cellular stress responses (SOD2, DUSP1), and cell cycle control/apoptosis (CDKN1A, BCL2A1). The high interconnectedness of hubs like FOS, TLR4, and CDKN1A highlights their central role in integrating these diverse cellular processes. An unexpected and consistent finding is the enrichment of viral infection pathways, suggesting that immune responses to pathogens or molecular mimicry may contribute to or exacerbate the chronic inflammation characteristic of PAD.
