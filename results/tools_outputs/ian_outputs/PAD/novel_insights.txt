The strong and consistent enrichment of pathways related to various viral infections (e.g., Hepatitis, Influenza, HIV, Coronavirus) is a significant and potentially novel finding in the context of PAD, suggesting an underappreciated role for viral mimicry or persistent viral activation in its pathogenesis.
The co-enrichment of these viral pathways with cancer-related pathways suggests a novel and complex interplay between infection, inflammation, and cellular transformation that may be relevant to PAD.
The potential regulatory role of RUNX2, a transcription factor primarily known for bone development, in vascular disease through its association with genes like ATF7IP and ANKRD46 warrants further investigation.
Similarly, the association of the transcription factor JUND with GPR15 and GK might represent a less-explored regulatory axis in PAD.
