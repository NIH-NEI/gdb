The strong enrichment of neuronal signaling pathways, such as the "Synaptic vesicle pathway" and "GABA and glutamate signalling," in peripheral blood transcriptomics of uveitis patients is a potentially novel finding, suggesting a more direct systemic link between inflammation and neuronal function than previously appreciated.
The co-enrichment of specific infectious disease pathways (e.g., Pertussis, COVID-19) alongside complement activation could suggest novel mechanisms where pathogens directly trigger or exacerbate complement-mediated inflammation in uveitis.
The significant differential expression of multiple Y-chromosome genes (UTY, KDM5D, DDX3Y, etc.) points to potentially novel sex-specific immune dysregulation or susceptibility in male uveitis patients.
The potential regulatory roles for transcription factors TFAP2C and POU5F1 in an inflammatory context, beyond their canonical functions, represent a potential new avenue for understanding the broader regulatory network in uveitis.
