Uveitis pathogenesis is driven by a complex interplay of immune dysregulation, extracellular matrix (ECM) remodeling, and potential neuronal involvement. The analysis reveals a system where hyperactive complement system activation and altered adaptive/innate immune responses lead to significant inflammation. This is compounded by substantial ECM remodeling, particularly involving the central hub gene FN1, which connects immune, complement, and cytoskeletal processes. Key transcription factors, including RELB and SMAD4, appear to orchestrate these interconnected events. The consistent enrichment of neuronal signaling pathways and the presence of Y-chromosome genes suggest a potential systemic link to neuronal function and possible sex-specific disease heterogeneity.
