The consistent and strong enrichment of pathways related to various viral and parasitic infections (e.g., Hepatitis C, COVID-19, African Trypanosomiasis, Malaria) in the context of membranous nephropathy is a significant finding, suggesting an underappreciated role for pathogen-associated molecular patterns or shared inflammatory mechanisms in the disease's pathogenesis.
The co-enrichment of microglia-related immune pathways and heme signaling pathways may represent novel neuro-immune and metabolic-inflammatory axes in this renal disease.
Specific regulatory interactions identified by ChEA, such as BACH1 targeting the ion channel KCNJ16 or FOXM1 targeting the receptor AXL, could represent novel regulatory mechanisms in membranous nephropathy that warrant further investigation.
