We hypothesize that the observed gene expression changes in membranous nephropathy patients reflect a systemic dysregulation of metabolic pathways and immune responses, driven by key upstream regulators like HNF1A, TGFB1, CYBB, and ALDOB. This dysregulation leads to altered glucose and amino acid metabolism, compromised immune cell function, and aberrant extracellular matrix remodeling, collectively contributing to the pathogenesis of membranous nephropathy.
