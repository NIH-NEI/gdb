The integrated analysis of membranous nephropathy reveals a pathology driven by a complex interplay between significant metabolic dysregulation and robust immune system activation. The system is characterized by profound alterations in amino acid, carbohydrate, and vitamin metabolism, orchestrated primarily by the master transcription factor HNF1A. This metabolic dysfunction is coupled with a strong inflammatory component, involving the complement system, immune cell activation (platelets, neutrophils), and signaling pathways like TGF-beta. Key hub proteins, including ALB, TP53, and TGFB1, act as central nodes integrating these metabolic and immune responses. The consistent, novel enrichment of viral and parasitic infection pathways suggests that host-pathogen interactions or shared inflammatory mechanisms may play an underappreciated role in exacerbating the disease.
