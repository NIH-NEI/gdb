The integrated analysis reveals that CD4+ PD-1+ T cells in early rheumatoid arthritis are in a state of heightened immune activation and significant proliferative capacity. This dual phenotype is driven by a convergence of pathways related to T cell activation, antigen presentation, and robust cell cycle progression. The system is orchestrated by key transcription factors, primarily MYB and FOXM1. MYB appears to regulate both immune signaling and cell cycle genes, while FOXM1 strongly promotes proliferation. The consistent enrichment of viral infection pathways suggests a novel role for viral mimicry or infection-induced signaling in exacerbating this T cell dysfunction. This active, proliferative state likely contributes significantly to the pro-inflammatory environment characteristic of early RA.
