In bullous pemphigoid, the disease phenotype is driven by a complex interplay of immune dysregulation, metabolic reprogramming, and cellular stress. The analysis highlights a significant involvement of inflammatory and immune response pathways, with key players including caspases (CASP1, CASP3, CASP5) and S100 proteins (S100A8, S100A9, S100A12). The master regulator SPI1 appears to orchestrate this broad immune response by activating innate pathways like NOD-like receptor signaling and inflammasomes. This is complemented by GATA3's influence on adaptive immunity, and P53/ATF3's role in mediating cellular stress and apoptosis. This inflammatory state is supported by significant metabolic alterations, particularly in fatty acid metabolism and mitochondrial function, which provide the necessary energy for these processes.
