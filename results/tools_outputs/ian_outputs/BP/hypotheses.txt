The observed changes in gene expression in peripheral blood from newly diagnosed, treatment-naive bullous pemphigoid patients reflect a systemic dysregulation of innate and adaptive immune responses, driven by key transcription factors. Specifically, we hypothesize that dysregulation of SPI1, GATA3, P53, and ATF3 leads to the aberrant activation of inflammasome pathways, pro-inflammatory cytokine signaling, and apoptotic processes, contributing to the systemic inflammatory state observed in early-stage BP. This dysregulation may also involve altered lipid metabolism, further exacerbating the inflammatory phenotype.
