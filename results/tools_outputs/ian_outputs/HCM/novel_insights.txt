The strong co-enrichment of metabolic reprogramming (specifically aerobic glycolysis and the Cori cycle) with immune system pathways (complement and leukocyte-targeted miRNAs) suggests a novel, intricate link between inflammation and metabolic shifts in the context of hypertrophic cardiomyopathy.
The potential regulation of extracellular matrix (COL16A1) and coagulation (F13A1) genes by the pluripotency factor SOX2 in adult cardiac tissue represents a potentially new regulatory axis in HCM pathogenesis.
The specific involvement of the nuclear receptor NR1H3 (LXRα) in regulating the inflammatory genes MAFB and ALOX5AP could be a less-explored mechanism of metabolic and inflammatory crosstalk within the hypertrophied heart.
