The observed changes in gene expression in heart tissues from hypertrophic cardiomyopathy cases reflect a dysregulation of cardiac muscle function, extracellular matrix remodeling, and inflammatory responses. Specifically, we hypothesize that aberrant activation or function of SMAD3 and SPI1 signaling pathways contributes to the development of HCM. SMAD3 dysregulation leads to altered expression of genes critical for cardiac contractility and structural integrity, as well as pathways involved in fibrosis and inflammation. Concurrently, SPI1 dysregulation promotes an inflammatory environment through the modulation of immune cell-related genes, exacerbating cardiac pathology.
