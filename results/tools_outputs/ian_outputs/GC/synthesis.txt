The integrated analysis reveals a complex interplay of altered extracellular matrix (ECM) organization, cell adhesion, metabolic reprogramming, and inflammation as key drivers of the gastric cancer phenotype. The system is characterized by a dysregulated ECM that promotes cell migration and invasion, supported by altered metabolic pathways that fuel cancer cell growth. Inflammation, mediated by key cytokines and enzymes, further contributes to the pro-tumorigenic microenvironment. This intricate network is likely orchestrated by a core set of transcription factors, including SOX2, RELA, GATA4, FOXA1, and KLF4, which regulate genes involved in these interconnected processes. Key themes include significant alterations in collagen and other ECM proteins, changes in cell adhesion molecules like integrins and claudins, and dysregulated metabolic pathways related to alcohol and xenobiotic metabolism.
