The observed differential expression of genes in gastric cancer tissues reflects a coordinated molecular program driven by key transcription factors, with SOX2 acting as a central effector. We hypothesize that dysregulation of transcription factors like KLF4 and FOXC1, along with altered expression of genes involved in epithelial integrity (e.g., MUC1, CLDNs) and ECM remodeling (e.g., COL1A1, SPP1), leads to the activation of SOX2. This activation of SOX2, in turn, orchestrates a cascade of downstream events that promote a pro-tumorigenic phenotype characterized by altered cell adhesion, increased invasiveness, and a modified tumor microenvironment.
